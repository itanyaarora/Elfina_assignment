Founder's Office Assignment · Part 1

Should Elfina build a referral channel,
and with whom?

A strategy memo on which referral partnership Elfina should build first.

Tanya Arora  ·  18 May 2026
01 · Recommendation

All three channels work.
Fertility clinics come first.

Psychiatrists Gynaecologists Fertility clinics

Psychiatrists, gynaecologists and fertility clinics are all valid referral partners over time. But they are not equal first bets. Given Elfina's size today, fertility clinics come first: the patients are exactly who Elfina is built for, the funnel is small enough to serve well, and it is concentrated enough for a lean team to reach.

02 · Who Elfina is for

The urban millennial professional.

Urban Educated 25–40 Pays out of pocket Already open to therapy Individual or couples

Elfina's own words name the customer: the founder calls them "working professionals," the company blurb a platform for "millennials in India." One detail to hold onto: Elfina matches couples, not only individuals. Every channel is judged on one question: does it deliver this person?

03 · Who is in each clinic

The three channels don't see the same patient.

Psychiatrist

A wide clinical spectrum

Anxiety and depression through to addiction and acute conditions. Medication-led, clinically complex, and most psychiatrists already work with their own therapists.

Gynaecologist

Real weight, rarely named

Women across reproductive life: pregnancy, postpartum, PCOS, menopause. The emotional weight is real but quietly under-discussed.

Fertility clinic

Couples, in a hard chapter

Couples going through IVF: urban, educated, mid-to-higher income. Already in an emotionally heavy chapter where support simply helps.

Only fertility-clinic patients are naturally ready for what Elfina offers. And, as couples, they fit the couples therapy Elfina already does.

05 · How we would go about it

Two-mode model. One 90-day pilot.

Clinic flags a struggling patient
→
Free matching call
→
Matched to a screened therapist
→
Ongoing therapy

The clinic owns the first step. Elfina owns the rest. Clinics without counselling take a goodwill referral; those that already counsel take Elfina as an escalation tier.

Land Days 1–30

Sign ART Fertility: premium, metro, no in-house counsellor. One city, 3–5 centres. Build the referral kit; train doctors + front-desk.

Target: pilot live in one city by day 30

Run Days 31–60

Doctors hand the card to distressed patients. Matching call within 24h. Weekly check-ins; loop-closing notes to referring doctors.

Target: 30+ referrals / centre / month · 25%+ call→session

Decide & scale Days 61–90

Review conversion per centre. Cut the dead ones, double down on the rest. If it clears the bar, expand to city #2.

Target: a clear go / no-go on city #2
06 · Risks & constraints

What I'd watch.

Competition

Half the major IVF chains already advertise in-house counselling, so the play is the half that don't. Elfina's wedge: matched clinical therapy, a tier deeper.

Time-bounded value

A fertility patient's therapy need fades when the journey ends, a shorter lifetime value than postpartum into parenting.

Legality

Indian medical-conduct rules prohibit paying for referrals. The model is a partnership, not a paid channel; money flows clinic-to-Elfina for a service delivered.

The economics

Everything rests on Elfina's revenue per patient and the matching-call conversion rate, the number to confirm before scaling.
